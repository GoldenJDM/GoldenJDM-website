I'm Amirhossein — most people online know me as GoldenJDM. I'm a student in Tehran who fell in love with the internet through two very different doors: a battered copy of Wikipedia and a grainy VHS of a Skyline GT-R hunting the Wangan at 3 a.m.

Since then, I've spent thousands of hours editing articles on the Persian and English Wikipedias — writing about cars, aviation history, engineers, and the small technical details most people scroll past. It taught me the discipline of citations, the ethics of neutrality, and the strange joy of fixing a broken sentence at midnight.

Beyond that, I build things — small, fast, honest websites. I care deeply about clean code, documentation nobody dreads reading, and pages that load before you finish blinking. In between, I read Hafez and Sohrab, watch NHK aviation documentaries, and argue politely about whether the R34 is truly the last real GT-R.
